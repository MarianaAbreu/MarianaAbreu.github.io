My PHD Experience

My PhD project was about a Multimodal Approach for Predicting and Monitoring Epilepsy. I thought I was going to seizures using cardiovascular activity, which was being done by other research teams, but with a great margin for improvement (in my opinion). There are so many techniques within the Artificial Intelligence realm, from reinforcement learning to transformers, to transfer learning neural networks used for other applicationsâ€¦
I thought I would spend my PhD testing all these methods and evaluating whether they would work in for epilepsy seizure prediction.

However, I found myself with two problems: data and complexity.

In epilepsy, few datasets contain peripheral data (data beyond EEG) and none existed when I started my PhD. Thus, I relied on self-collected data, supervising the acquisition of 60 patients for 3 years, and expanding from 1 epilepsy monitoring unit (EMU) to another, by managing the contact between the institute and the hospital.
