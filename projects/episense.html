Cardiorespiratory wearable monitoring in epilepsy:
prospects and challenges

Introduction

Cardiorespiratory changes in epilepsy are very common and dangerous if not detected in time. While these autonomic phenomena may be undetected in smartwatch-based seizure devices, chest-worn wearable monitoring arises as a solution. The Electrocardiogram (ECG) allows to capture cardiovascular dysfunctions, such as sinus tachycardia, whereas respiratory dysfunctions, such as apnea, may be better described by the Respiration (RESP) signal. This work consisted in the analysis of the wearable data collection during in-hospital visits of patients with epilepsy for video-EEG monitoring.

Data Quality Assessment

  • ECG Quality Analysis was measured in 3 levels: low quality (0), medium quality (0.5) and High Quality (1)
  • RESP Quality Analysis was measured in 2 levels: low quality (0) and high quality (1)
  • ACC Quality is not so straightforward, thus the Activity Index allows to understand the motion range.
